# Phase 2: Methodological Framework Addendum
# Semantic Legitimacy: Standards for Human-Aligned Output

## 1. Objective
To mitigate the "robotic" and hyper-predictable signatures of machine-assisted knowledge production, ensuring that all Grossi School intellectual outputs possess the "Linguistic Fingerprint" of authentic human reasoning.

## 2. Key Metrics
### 2.1. Perplexity (Unpredictability)
Outputs must bypass the low-perplexity threshold of AI detectors by utilizing:
*   **Creative Metaphors:** Moving beyond generic idioms to original analogies.
*   **Contextual Nuance:** Injecting unique personal or institutional insights that a generalized model cannot replicate.
*   **Linguistic Variety:** Intentionally selecting low-probability (but high-accuracy) synonyms for common "AI-native" terms (e.g., swapping "delve into" for "explore").

### 2.2. Burstiness (Rhythmic Variation)
The rhythm of the text must reflect the non-linear nature of human thought:
*   **Sentence Length Variance:** Proactive mixing of short, declarative statements with complex, multi-clausal sentences.
*   **Structural Inversion:** Utilizing subordinate clauses and prepositional phrases at the start of sentences to break the "Subject-Verb-Object" monotony.

## 3. The Humanization Algorithm
All institutional text must undergo a four-step "Humanization Pass":

1.  **Structural Edit:** Break the rhythm; combine and split sentences for dynamic flow.
2.  **Vocabulary Swap:** Direct removal of "Hedge Phrases" and "Formal Buzzwords" (Refer to the AI Vocabulary Matrix).
3.  **Personalization Pass:** Injection of concrete data, specific dates, and "Point-of-View" statements.
4.  **Acoustic Polish:** Reading the text aloud to ensure natural cadence and "Human-Locked" tone.

---
**Institutional Standard:** All Grossi School courses must adhere to a >75% "Human Probability" score on recognized semantic audit tools.
